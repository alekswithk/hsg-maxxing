Agents arrive one at a time. Each sees their own private signal (accuracy q) and the public actions of everyone before them — never their signals. Watch how rational agents decode early actions, then stop using their own signal once the public lead reaches |Na − Nb| = 2. From that point a cascade locks in and every later private signal is wasted. Add agents randomly, or hand-feed signals to force a wrong cascade.

Cascade active. Every new agent now copies the herd regardless of their own signal, so their private information never enters the public record. Aggregation has stopped — the herd will stay here forever unless a contrarian signal is publicly revealed.

A planner can change what information is public. Compare three regimes on two welfare metrics. Note the two counter-intuitive results from the lecture: a more accurate public signal (q′ > q) can raise the error rate by crowding out social learning, and removing cascades (short recall) lowers average correctness even as it removes disaster.
